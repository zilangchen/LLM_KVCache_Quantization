\begin{abstracten}

As long-context inference expands, the KV cache in autoregressive decoding keeps increasing with sequence length. In practical deployment, GPU memory capacity gradually becomes a limiting factor for serving large models. Reducing cache bit-width directly lowers memory footprint, but tensor-level reconstruction error does not fully describe the functional shift after a quantized cache enters attention. Key perturbations may reshape attention distributions through logits and softmax, while Value perturbations affect output representations through weighted aggregation. This thesis proposes a behavior-aligned KV-cache quantization framework for efficient inference. It uses attention distributions, aggregation outputs, and task behavior as shared calibration criteria, connecting quantization-parameter selection, low-bit recovery, and quantization-budget allocation.

The method evaluates full-precision reference configurations and candidate quantized configurations on calibration samples. It records attention-distribution KL and attention-aggregation output perturbation as two behavioral readings for calibration-parameter selection, while retaining downstream task scores as task-level audit evidence. The layer-wise readings obtained during calibration are further summarized into hierarchical behavioral sensitivity and organized across models as a sensitivity-profile map, which reveals how critical layers are distributed under different model conditions. This map is then used to compare behavior-guided allocation with positional heuristics under fixed-$k$ budgets. The proposed \texttt{AutoK} further generates model-level $k$ candidates from sensitivity rankings and coverage thresholds. As a result, one set of offline behavioral readings supports fidelity calibration, low-bit behavior recovery, cross-model structural interpretation, and budget candidate generation.

Experiments cover six open-source instruction models from the Qwen2.5, LLaMA-3.1, and Mistral families. The results show that KV-cache quantization and budget allocation depend clearly on model family, scale, and task. Behavior-guided calibration preserves stable fidelity under conservative \texttt{INT8} bit-width. After moving to \texttt{INT4}, symmetric quantization triggers stepwise retrieval collapse on Qwen models, and K/V diagnostics show that Key-side low-bit noise is the direct instability source. The role-aware low-bit path \texttt{INT4-RoleAlign} restores retrieval behavior to a usable range. In cross-model budget experiments, \texttt{AutoK} shows its strongest effect on Mistral-7B. Qwen2.5-3B corresponds to an early-layer protection regime, LLaMA-3.1-8B shows a medium-scale consensus regime, and Qwen2.5-14B presents nearby high-performance clusters without a stable winner. The \texttt{INT4} path yields about $73.4\%$ KV-cache capacity compression, while fused-decode time gains are jointly affected by $H_{kv}$, sequence length, and backend implementation. Overall, this thesis validates a behavior-aligned KV-cache quantization framework centered on attention-behavior preservation, K/V role-aware low-bit recovery, and \texttt{AutoK} with behavioral sensitivity profiles as the budget interface.

\end{abstracten}
