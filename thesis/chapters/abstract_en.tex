\begin{abstracten}

Long-context autoregressive decoding keeps an increasingly long history of keys and values, so KV-cache capacity and memory traffic grow with sequence length. Lowering cache bit-width directly reduces this pressure, but the quantized cache is eventually consumed by attention rather than evaluated only as a tensor reconstruction problem. Key-side perturbations pass through logits and softmax before changing attention mass, whereas Value-side perturbations enter the output through weighted aggregation. The thesis builds a behavior-aligned KV-cache quantization framework around these two propagation paths, using attention distributions, aggregated outputs, and task behavior to guide calibration, low-bit recovery, and layer-wise budget allocation.

The method first runs full-precision reference and candidate quantized configurations on the same calibration samples. Attention-distribution KL and aggregation-output perturbation provide the main fidelity readings, while downstream task scores remain as task-level audit signals. The layer-wise readings are then summarized into behavioral sensitivity profiles and compared across models as a sensitivity-profile map. This map supports fixed-$k$ comparisons between behavior-guided allocation, positional heuristics, and uniform allocation, and \texttt{AutoK} uses the same rankings with coverage thresholds to produce model-level $k$ candidates. The offline readings therefore continue beyond calibration and become the interface for low-bit recovery, cross-model interpretation, and budget selection.

Experiments on six open-source instruction models from Qwen2.5, LLaMA-3.1, and Mistral show strong family, scale, and task dependence. On Qwen2.5-1.5B, the INT8 baseline differs from \texttt{FP16} by about $+0.02$ in the task-core three-task mean, giving a stable conservative-bit-width reference. Under \texttt{INT4}, symmetric quantization triggers stepwise retrieval collapse on Qwen models, and K/V diagnostics point to Key-side low-bit noise as the more direct instability source. \texttt{INT4-RoleAlign} restores retrieval to a usable range. Cross-model allocation further separates four regimes, with the clearest \texttt{AutoK} case on Mistral-7B, early-layer protection on Qwen2.5-3B, a medium-scale consensus regime on LLaMA-3.1-8B, and nearby high-performance clusters without a stable winner on Qwen2.5-14B. The \texttt{INT4} path reduces KV-cache capacity by about $73.4\%$, while fused-decode time gains depend on $H_{kv}$, sequence length, and backend implementation. These results move KV-cache quantization from uniform bit-width reduction toward an auditable behavior-alignment problem.

\end{abstracten}
