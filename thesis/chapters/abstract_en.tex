\begin{abstracten}

As large language models are increasingly deployed in long-context question answering, document understanding, and complex reasoning tasks, the memory cost of the key-value (KV) cache during inference has become a major obstacle to efficient deployment. Existing quantization methods are usually calibrated with numerical reconstruction objectives such as mean squared error or percentile-based clipping, which do not directly capture how quantization noise affects attention behavior after the softmax nonlinearity.

To address this issue, we propose a behavior-aligned quantization framework that uses the KL divergence between full-precision and quantized attention distributions as both a calibration objective and a diagnostic lens. At the INT8 level, the framework combines adaptive scale protection with fused Triton decode-attention kernels, achieving no statistically significant degradation in PPL, Needle, or LongBench across four models from three model families (with moderate RULER retrieval-subtask degradation of $-2.64\%$ discussed in Chapter~4), together with approximately 44\% KV-cache memory savings and 8\%--38\% decode latency reduction. At the INT4 level, the KL diagnostic lens reveals the systematic failure mode of symmetric per-group quantization, and controlled K/V ablations identify Key quantization as the dominant source of degradation. Guided by this diagnosis, we further propose INT4-RoleAlign: adopting per-channel Key and per-token Value asymmetric quantization axes, with BA-guided percentile calibration to search for optimal K/V percentile parameters.

Experiments show that INT4-RoleAlign restores the Needle pass rate from 0\% to 100\% on Qwen2.5-1.5B, reducing PPL degradation from 12\% (KIVI-style) to 1.2\%, and demonstrates competitiveness against KIVI-style INT4 across three models (two model families). Under the fully quantized evaluation protocol (chunk\_size=1) on Qwen2.5-1.5B, KIVI-style PPL degrades catastrophically to 9{,}442 while INT4-RoleAlign remains at 9.49, exhibiting robustness to quantization evaluation granularity on the evaluated model. This work contributes not only a calibration criterion better aligned with model behavior, but also demonstrates attention-KL as a diagnostic lens guiding quantization design evolution from symmetric to asymmetric formats and from INT8 to INT4.

\end{abstracten}
