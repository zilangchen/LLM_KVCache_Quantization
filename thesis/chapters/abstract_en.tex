\begin{abstracten}

As large language model context windows scale from thousands
to hundreds of thousands of tokens, key-value (KV) cache memory
grows linearly with sequence length and has become the primary
bottleneck for long-context inference deployment.
INT4 quantization is an attractive compression route,
theoretically reducing KV cache to one quarter of its size,
yet calibrating with numerical reconstruction objectives
cannot explain the catastrophic failure observed under symmetric
INT4 at long contexts: Needle retrieval drops from 100\% to 0\%
and perplexity degrades by up to roughly 14\% on the most sensitive
models.

Our starting point is that KV cache quantization should preserve
attention behavior rather than minimize numerical reconstruction
error. We therefore propose attention-KL as a dual-purpose
criterion---it drives the offline search for quantization parameters
on the one hand, and on the other, when quantization fails, the
same divergence lets us trace where the failure occurs and why.
This places calibration and post-hoc diagnosis under a single
behavior-aligned objective.

The principle yields the core contribution of this work:
INT4-RoleAlign, a diagnosis-derived role-aware low-bit design
with role-differentiated quantization axes (per-channel Key plus
per-token Value) and behavior-aligned percentile calibration.
INT4-RoleAlign recovers Needle retrieval from the 0\% symmetric-INT4
baseline back to 100\% (matching FP16) across three models
(Qwen2.5-1.5B/7B and LLaMA-3.1-8B) at 73\% KV cache compression
relative to FP16 (the residual 2\% reflects per-channel scale and
zero-point overhead).
PPL on wikitext-2 degrades inversely with the GQA head count:
2.4\% at $H_{\text{kv}}{=}8$, 6.1\% at $H_{\text{kv}}{=}4$, and
13.7\% at $H_{\text{kv}}{=}2$. The contrast indicates that local
retrieval tolerates INT4 quantization noise far better than global
language modeling.

The design is reached through a diagnostic chain.
We first validate the principle on INT8: behavior-aligned calibration
preserves quality on Qwen2.5-1.5B/7B (PPL within 0.3\%, Needle 100\%)
while reducing KV cache memory by roughly 44\% and decode latency
by 8--38\%; calibration sensitivity on LLaMA-3.1-8B is disclosed as
a known limitation in Chapter 4. The same diagnostic lens then
reveals the structural root cause of symmetric INT4 failure:
controlled K/V ablation across three model families consistently
identifies Key quantization as the dominant quality loss factor,
with a pronounced dependence on the GQA architecture. This points
directly at the role-aware design. INT4-RoleAlign shares the
per-channel Key / per-token Value format with KIVI but replaces
KIVI's runtime absmax estimation by an offline attention-KL
percentile search, shifting the calibration signal from numerical
reconstruction to attention-behavior fidelity.

The diagnostic process also surfaced an unexpected regularity.
Inverse temperature correction (inv\_tau) exhibits a strong
scale-dependence on GQA heads: it improves PPL by 1.6\% at
$H_{\text{kv}}{=}2$ but degrades PPL by 3.4--6.0\% at
$H_{\text{kv}}{\geq}4$. The pattern is qualitatively consistent
with a heuristic GQA noise-dilution argument, in which noise
from a shared KV head appears attenuated by downstream aggregation;
a rigorous derivation would require i.i.d.\ assumptions that do
not strictly hold under GQA and is left as future work. We stress
that this was not a design target but something that fell out of
the same KL parameter sweep.

Behavior-aligned calibration, in sum, is more than a better
quantization objective. It identifies where INT4 breaks, shapes
the asymmetric design that follows, and makes clear where that
design stops working. All numbers above are averaged over five
seeds of the main experiments (with an additional ten-seed run to
verify PPL determinism), reported with Bootstrap confidence
intervals under BH-FDR multiple-comparison correction.

\end{abstracten}
