\begin{abstracten}

As large language model context windows scale from thousands
to hundreds of thousands of tokens, KV cache memory grows
linearly with sequence length and has become the primary
bottleneck for long-context inference deployment. INT4
quantization can in principle reduce KV cache memory to
$1/4$ of its original size, yet existing methods calibrated
with numerical reconstruction error degrade sharply at long
contexts: Needle retrieval drops from 100\% to 0\% and
perplexity degrades by over 15\%. This paper revisits the
calibration objective from an attention-behavior perspective,
using the Kullback--Leibler divergence between pre- and
post-quantization attention distributions (attention-KL) as
a unified calibration and diagnostic principle, with GQA
head count $H_{kv}$ as a structural correlate linking
calibration sensitivity, degradation patterns, and fused
decoding efficiency.

Across Qwen2.5-1.5B/7B/14B and LLaMA-3.1-8B (with 14B serving
as an external-validity anchor, evaluated only on 1 seed over
Needle 4K--32K and RULER 4K--16K and therefore excluded from
the PPL monotonicity claim), results show that calibration
objective validity is both bit-width and scale dependent:
at INT8 the two objectives converge, while at INT4 they
diverge on a small model ($H_{kv}=2$) but reconverge on a
larger one ($H_{kv}=4$). Controlled diagnosis shows INT4
degradation is Key-dominated: on the 14B model, preserving
Key in FP16 recovers 93\% of the PPL degradation, while
Needle retrieval and PPL decouple consistently across all
four models under the INT4-RoleAlign configuration (other
INT4 configurations such as symmetric-only exhibit Needle
drops that track PPL degradation). This diagnosis motivates
a per-channel Key $+$ per-token Value asymmetric format---the
same format independently proposed by KIVI~\cite{liu2024kivi}
from engineering observations. Building on this format,
INT4-RoleAlign is instantiated via offline attention-KL
search. Across the three models with full quality matrices
(1.5B/7B/8B), RoleAlign preserves 100\% Needle retrieval at
roughly 73\% KV cache compression, with PPL degradation
decreasing with scale (1.5B: 13.7\%; 7B: 6.1\%; 8B: 2.4\%).
The 14B anchor extends the Needle/RULER evidence to a larger
model but reports a PPL degradation of 7.6\%, which departs
from the 1.5B$\to$8B monotonic trend and is treated as a
supplementary external-validity observation rather than part
of the main trend. Relative to the KIVI-style baseline,
INT4-RoleAlign does not exhibit a consistent PPL advantage;
its contribution lies in providing an interpretable offline
calibration interface on the per-channel K $+$ per-token V
format via the attention-KL diagnostic lens, rather than in
raw quantization quality. The efficiency gains of fused
quantized decoding kernels show scale-dependent behaviour in
the $(H_{kv}, \text{seq\_len})$ space: at $H_{kv}=8$ the
fused kernel reduces latency by roughly 40\% at 32K, while
at $H_{kv}=2$ it is slower than SDPA across all tested
sequence lengths.

\end{abstracten}
