\begin{abstracten}

Long-context inference in large language models increasingly moves the decoding bottleneck from computation to the storage and memory-bandwidth cost of the growing KV cache. Reducing cache bit-width lowers memory footprint, but tensor-level reconstruction error is not sufficient to characterize functional fidelity once the quantized cache is consumed by attention. Key perturbations can reshape logits and softmax attention distributions, whereas Value perturbations mainly propagate through weighted aggregation into output representations. To address this problem, this thesis proposes a behavior-aligned KV-cache quantization framework for efficient LLM inference. The framework treats attention distributions, aggregation outputs, and task behavior as shared calibration and auditing targets, connecting quantization-parameter selection, low-bit recovery, and layer-wise budget allocation.

The framework builds an INT8 canonical path calibrated with an attention-distribution KL proxy, providing a reproducible fidelity anchor under a conservative bit-width. It then introduces \texttt{INT4-RoleAlign}, a role-aware asymmetric path that uses per-channel Key and per-token Value formats to characterize Key-side attention-ranking shifts and Value-side output perturbations separately, rather than collapsing both error channels into a single reconstruction objective. The same offline calibration artifacts are summarized into layer-wise behavioral sensitivity profiles for behavior-guided fixed-$k$ allocation, positional heuristic comparison, and \texttt{AutoK} coverage-based budget proposals. This design yields a consistent evidence chain from calibration to allocation.

Experiments across six open-source instruction models from the Qwen2.5, LLaMA-3.1, and Mistral families show that KV-cache quantization and allocation are strongly family-, scale-, and task-dependent. On Qwen2.5-1.5B, the INT8 canonical path differs from FP16 by approximately $+0.02$ in the three-task mean, supporting its role as a conservative fidelity case. Symmetric INT4 produces stepwise retrieval collapse on Qwen models, and K/V role diagnostics identify low-bit Key noise as the more direct instability trigger. In cross-model allocation, Mistral-7B provides the clearest single-model support for \texttt{AutoK}, with a core mean of 14.76 and an extend mean of 15.69; Qwen2.5-3B reveals an early-layer protection regime, where behavior-guided $k=1$ reaches a core mean of 6.90 compared with 3.48 for the middle-layer heuristic; and Qwen2.5-14B shows no stable winner, with Uniform INT4 and AutoK-cov90 reaching core means of 7.23 and 7.15. System measurements further indicate an approximately 73.4\% KV-cache capacity reduction for the INT4 path on four representative models, while fused-decode speedups depend on $H_{kv}$, sequence length, and backend implementation. Overall, this thesis formulates KV-cache quantization as a structured problem of preserving attention behavior, respecting K/V role asymmetry, and allocating precision under model- and task-dependent regimes.

\end{abstracten}
